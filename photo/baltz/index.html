Something about Lewis Baltz's flat, minimal style brought me great satisfaction from the moment I first saw his images. A key artist in the New Topographics exhibition, Baltz was one of the first to challenge the norms of landscape photography. There is a plainness to his work that sets it apart.

This set of images is my attempt to mimic Baltz's stark, geometric aesthetic. I wished to continue his exploration of manmade landscapes by photographing the many engineering labs and industrial facilities scattered on the northern outskirts of Oklahoma State University. They are named in a pattern similar to that of Baltz, with cardinal directions and building identifiers gathered from OSU's geospatial systems. So if you want to visit these exact buildings and see their marvelous austerity for yourself, you can!
