A meditation on the idea that every digital photograph is a translation from the physical to the numerical, this series considers the inverse of this process by persuading a computer to interpret other data files as though they were compiled in an image format. Files including the full text of The Bible and a recording of Beethoven's Moonlight Sonata were converted to grayscale images and systematically embedded into the RGB channels of other digital photographs. The results were saved in a lossless format, making it possible to download the pictures and recover the documents in their original states.
