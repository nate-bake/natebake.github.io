A direct extension of Hiroshi Sugimoto's well-known Theatres, this project employs prolonged exposures to obscure the contents of dynamic displays, and explore the pervasive presence of technology in an indivdiual's personal habitat. By isolating various "smart" devices as the sole sources of light, these images emphasize the extent to which modern beings depend on such devices and the software inside them.

The initial trajectory of this project was to closely mirror Sugimoto's approach by photographing vast, empty lecture halls on the Oklahoma State University campus. However, after capturing just two of these spaces, the scope of this endeavor was greatly confined by an emerging global pandemic. The additional constraints of quarantine forced me to consider the more intimate spaces in which screens lurk, not just large educational ones — a shift which resulted in a blend of familiarity and eeriness that I did not foresee.
