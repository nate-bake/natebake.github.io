Rocket League AI

By far my biggest programming project that I have embarked on outside of school has been to create a bot that can learn to play Rocket League by analyzing replays of human gameplay.

Rocket League is a competitive physics-based game of soccer with cars that has a very high skill ceiling. Even experienced players struggle to perform well consistently. This is just one of many reasons why the vast majority of bot creators opt for hardcoded routines rather than machine learning. When I started this project I was not aware that many brilliant members of the RLBot community had already concluded that it is not feasible to train a model well on human replay data.

Thus, I began writing scripts to collect and process thousands of publicly available replay files, and after many months of programming and learning about neural networks, I feel I have seen some encouraging results. So on this page I will try to explain the approach I took, documenting my failures, successes, and work that remains to be done.

If you can just want to see the code, you can find most of it in this repository.
